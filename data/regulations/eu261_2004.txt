Regulation (EC) No 261/2004

Article 5 - Cancellation
In case of cancellation, passengers shall be offered assistance and may be entitled to compensation under the conditions of this Regulation.

Article 6 - Delay
When an operating air carrier reasonably expects a flight to be delayed beyond specified thresholds, passengers are entitled to assistance.

Article 7 - Right to compensation
Where reference is made to this Article, passengers shall receive compensation of EUR 250, EUR 400, or EUR 600, depending on flight distance and route conditions.

Article 8 - Right to reimbursement or re-routing
Passengers shall be offered reimbursement or re-routing options in accordance with this Regulation.

Article 9 - Right to care
Passengers shall be offered meals, refreshments, communication, and accommodation where required.

Article 14 - Obligation to inform passengers
Air carriers shall ensure passengers are informed of their rights.

Recital / Case-law Note
CJEU case law commonly treats arrival delay of 3 hours or more similarly to cancellation for compensation analysis, subject to extraordinary circumstances.

Article 5(3) - Extraordinary circumstances
Compensation may be denied where the carrier proves extraordinary circumstances which could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken.
